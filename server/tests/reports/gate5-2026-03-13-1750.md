**Agente retornado:** FLEX

**Tempo:** 28793ms

**Resposta:**

> Hey there! Great question about the Present Perfect! It's super useful for talking about things that happened in the past, but are still connected to now, or about experiences you've had in your life.
> 
> Think of it like this:
> 
> 👉 We use **Simple Past** for actions that started and finished at a specific time in the past. It's like a completed story. For example: "I **watched** a great movie yesterday." (Eu assisti um ótimo filme ontem.) The action is done, and we know *when*.
> 
> 👉 We use **Present Perfect** for actions that started in the past but are still true now, or actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past and have a connection to the present. It's like an ongoing story or an experience. For example: "I **have watched** that movie many times." (Eu assisti/tenho assistido esse filme muitas vezes.) Here, the action happened in the past, but it's not about *when*, it's about the *experience* up to now.
> 
> So, Simple Past = specific time, finished action. Present Perfect = unspecified time, or action connected to the present.

> 
> → Usem exemplos do cotidiano, como em letras de músicas ou em descrições de jogos: "O personagem é *forte como* um touro" (comparação) versus "O personagem *é* um touro" (metáfora).
